Chapter 04

The Architecture of Shadows

The dawn did not break; it simply seeped through the heavy curtains like a slow, grey ink. Elias sat at the mahogany desk, his fingers tracing the indentations left by a hundred letters written and never sent. Outside, the city of Aethelgard breathed with the mechanical precision of a clockwork heart, but inside this room, time felt liquid and unreliable.

He turned the page of the manuscript. The ink was fresh, shimmering slightly in the dim morning light. It spoke of a place that shouldn't exist—a bridge built not of stone or iron, but of memories woven into a physical span. "To cross," the text whispered in his mind, "is to lose who you were, to become who you once hoped to be."

A sudden knock at the door shattered the silence. Not the polite rap of a servant, but the heavy, rhythmic thud of authority. Elias knew they had found the bridge. Or rather, they had found the map that led to it. He looked at the fireplace, then at the manuscript. The choice was a weight he wasn't sure his tired shoulders could carry anymore.

The shadows in the corner of the room seemed to lengthen, detaching themselves from the floor. They weren't just absences of light anymore; they were structures, pillars of darkness supporting the ceiling. The architecture of the room was changing, responding to the fear vibrating through his chest. He realized then that the bridge wasn't in some distant forest. It was here, beginning in the very core of his residence.
